## Is It Actually Safe?

This is the real question people have, and it's fair. The short answer is yes, with the usual caveats that come with any bandwidth-sharing app.

Honeygain uses bandwidth encryption, doesn't access your personal data or files, and has passed antivirus reviews from McAfee, Windows Defender, and Avast. The company applies KYC (Know Your Customer) standards to vet the businesses that use the network — they're not just selling your IP to whoever shows up.

The honest caveat: you're allowing a third party to route traffic through your connection. That's a trust trade-off. For most home users with standard residential internet, the practical risk is low. If you're on a metered data plan or have unusually strict ISP terms, worth reading the fine print first. Also check whether your credits are safe long-term: active accounts don't expire, but inactive ones lose credits after 6 months.
